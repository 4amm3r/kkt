Whole-hearted Cat Care

We strive to develop a heart-to-heart connection with your kitty, as we feel that this emotional bond allows them to feel safe, comfortable, and loved.

No Time Limits

All visits (except cat sitting by the hour) have no time limit. We take great care in everything we do, and we take the time to pamper your kitty with the attention they desire.

Timely Updates

You can have peace of mind while you are out of town, as we send you daily updates with pictures of your furry family member(s).

Professional Qualifications

Licensed, bonded, and insured and certified in pet CPR and first aid. We are a member of the National Association of Professional Pet Sitters (NAPPS).

Meet and Greet

Before we start any services, we schedule a 30-60 minute consultation in your home to go over your pet's routine and address any questions you may have. We will obtain two keys from you at this time and will go over home care as well. We will bring forms and leave them with you to fill out later. We pick these forms up with payment on our first visit.
$20 for each meeting

Key Service

During the meet and greet, we will obtain two keys from you to keep on file. Your keys are labeled with only your pet's name. If you are not comfortable with us safeguarding your keys, you may either drop off/pick up the keys from us, you can purchase a key lock box and provide us with the access code and location, or we can drive to your home for a fee:
$15 per trip

About

  • My name is Nadia

    I am the owner of Kitty Kat Time. Ever since I was little, animals have been my passion! I grew up with frogs, fish, and guinea pigs.

  • Snowflake

    I got my first cat when I was 6 and named her Snowflake. She was a gorgeous part Siamese/tabby cat. She experienced a lot in her 16 years on earth and even moved 3,000 miles across the country with me. Snowflake started my love for cats.

  • Volunteering

    In high school I began volunteering at cat shelters. I was a cat care specialist volunteer for the Northwest Organization for Animal Help (NOAH) and volunteered with the Everett Animal Shelter as a cat room assistant. I also volunteered at Pasado’s Safe Haven and at Sarvey Wildlife Care, helping to rehabilitate wild animals.

  • Work Experience

    I got my Bachelor of Science degree in Animal Behavior and then became an independent contractor with two pet service companies, providing cat/small animal visits, overnight pet care, dog walking, and puppy training. I have experience with farm animals from working on a horse farm and interning at Farm Sanctuary’s Animal Acres.

  • My Cat

    I currently have one cat named Salie.
